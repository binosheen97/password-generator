🛡️ Password Strength Guide

Weak

Less than 8 chars or only one type. Crackable in seconds.

Fair

8-11 chars with 2 types. Crackable in hours to days.

Good

12-15 chars with 3 types. Resistant to most attacks.

Strong

16+ chars with all types. Virtually uncrackable.

💡 Password Safety Tips

  • Use a different password for every account
  • Use a password manager (Bitwarden, 1Password)
  • Never share your password with anyone
  • Enable 2-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Change passwords after any data breach
  • Avoid personal info (name, birthday, phone)
  • Never use common passwords (123456, password)

🔒 How Secure is This?

All passwords are generated entirely in your browser using JavaScript's crypto.getRandomValues() — a cryptographically secure random number generator.

We never store, transmit, or log any generated passwords. Your passwords never leave your device.

Why Use a Password Generator?

Humans are predictable when creating passwords. We use names, dates, and common words that hackers can easily guess. A random password generator creates truly unpredictable passwords.

  • Protects against brute force attacks
  • Protects against dictionary attacks
  • Unique passwords prevent credential stuffing
  • Long random passwords are virtually uncrackable

How Long Should a Password Be?

  • Minimum 12 characters for basic accounts
  • 16+ characters for email & social media
  • 20+ characters for banking & sensitive accounts
  • Longer is always better — use 16+ as your default
  • Include all character types for maximum security
